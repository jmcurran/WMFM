# Stage 45.5: Comparable-observation foundation

Stage 45.5 replaces the previous blanket conditional-quantile boundary for suitable questions such as "What is a good deal for a one carat diamond?" with a deterministic comparable-observation pathway.

The stage:

- classifies explicit comparable-case and good-deal questions;
- reuses the existing prediction-value parser and deterministic completion rules;
- computes mixed-predictor distances from the fitted model frame;
- range-standardises numeric predictor differences;
- represents factor differences as matches or mismatches;
- retains the nearest fitted observations and their observed responses; and
- records explicit limitations without yet producing a bargain judgement or polished explanation.

Stage 45.6 should turn this structured result into calibrated deterministic prose. Stage 45.7 should provide an inspectable comparable-case display.
